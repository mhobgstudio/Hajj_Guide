What is Hajj?

Hajj is the annual Islamic pilgrimage to Makkah, Saudi Arabia, and is one of the Five Pillars of Islam.

When is Hajj?

Hajj occurs during the Islamic month of Dhu al-Hijjah and involves a series of rituals performed over several days.

Who Must Perform Hajj?

Hajj is obligatory once in a lifetime for every adult Muslim who is physically and financially able.

Types of Hajj

Type Description Umrah Sacrifice
Tamattu' Perform Umrah during Hajj months, exit Ihram, then re-enter for Hajj Yes Yes
Qiran Perform Umrah and Hajj together without exiting Ihram Yes Yes
Ifrad Perform only Hajj (no Umrah) No No

1. Preparation & Intention

Make the sincere intention to perform Hajj solely for the sake of Allah.

2. Enter State of Ihram

At the Miqat, don the special white garments and recite the Talbiyah.

3. Perform Umrah

Tawaf, Sa'i, and shaving/trimming hair.

4. Day at Mina

Spend the day and night in prayer at Mina.

5. Day at Arafat

Stand in prayer and supplication from noon until sunset.

6. Muzdalifah & Stoning

Collect pebbles and perform the stoning ritual.

7. Sacrifice & Tawaf

Offer sacrifice and perform Tawaf al-Ifadah.

8. Farewell Tawaf

Perform final Tawaf before leaving Makkah.

Important Places

  • Masjid al-Haram - Mecca
  • Kaaba - House of Allah
  • Mount Arafat
  • Mina - Tent City
  • Muzdalifah
  • Jamarat - Stoning Pillars

Is visiting Madinah part of Hajj?

No, visiting Madinah is not a Hajj requirement but is highly recommended.

Who is exempt from Hajj?

Those who are not physically or financially able are exempt.

What is Transfer Hajj?

Appointing someone to perform Hajj on another's behalf if unable to perform it.

Important Reminders

Hajj is one of the five pillars of Islam and is obligatory for every Muslim who has the physical and financial capability to perform it at least once in their lifetime.

Please ensure you follow all Hajj rules and regulations set by the Saudi authorities.

Prepare physically and spiritually before undertaking the journey.

Learn the supplications and rituals in advance.

Maintain patience and humility throughout the pilgrimage.
